Luther Network

The Story Of Why "Rate My Caf" Was Only Up For Three Days

Start Reading

"Rate My Caf" was banned of the Luther network

The Explanation

When "Rate My Caf" was first launch, it didn't have much of a following by any means. It was a very small app that I showed a couple of friends, toyed around with, and learned from. Sure, I wanted it expand and grow in users, but during the the first couple days I wanted to just test it. Building full stack applications is still relatively new to me, so I wanted to make sure everything ran smoothly. And for the most part, it got good feedback besides to a couple of UI changes.

And that's when it got taken down.

At first I was frustrated, not because I thought it got taken down, but because earlier that week I was have trouble connecting the domain to the actual website which was infuriating. But when I realized that it wasn't on my end and that Luther had actually taken it down, I was scared shitless. I thought the Luther ITS team was going to bust through my dorm door, through a bag over my head, and take me out to a rural corn field outside of Decorah and have me whacked. Obviously I'm joking, but nonetheless it was a bit anxious that I had done something wrong.

However, this quick 15 seconds of panic and horror was almost immediately diminished when I started to take a little pride in its removal. After all, Luther College has officially acknowledged my website. I thought it was kinda cool. Granted I made the website for Luther students and staff to use, so then it was time to try and get it back.

That was probably the hardest part; getting in touch with the Luther Networking Team and ITS. It took about three weeks before I even a response back from either party, but once I finally got an email back I was asked to meet with the head of Dining Services. And that went really well! I walked them through the app and they seemed to have enjoyed it! Overall, it worked out really well and now the app is back up!
